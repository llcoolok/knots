Candlestand

2022

In candlestand, I forgo hardware in favor of integrated threads and traditional joinery, creating a structure that is both adaptable and easily disassembled. This approach allows the piece to respond to its user, embracing transformation as a core function.

Candlestand draws on references to 17th- and 18th-century tables designed to illuminate their own surfaces, reinterpreting these historical forms through a contemporary lens.

Charcoal and graphite on paper

60 × 80 cm

Coastal Dwelling

2023

This work examines the intersection of human-made structures and natural environments. The black house, standing stoically against the coastal landscape, represents the human desire to both embrace and control nature.

The stark contrast between the geometric forms of architecture and the organic shapes of the surrounding environment creates a dialogue about permanence and impermanence.

Silver gelatin print

40 × 40 cm

Natural Forms

2022

This collection of found objects explores the sculptural qualities inherent in natural forms. Each object—selected for its unique shape, texture, and history—offers a meditation on the artistic processes of nature itself.

By removing these objects from their original context and presenting them as art, the work questions the boundaries between natural artifact and artistic creation.

Mixed media installation with found objects

Dimensions variable
